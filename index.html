Fast Food Consumption and Obesity in Australia

According to the ABS criteria, people with a Body Mass Index (BMI) of 25+ are considered to be overweight or obese. Australia ranks 10th out of 21 countries for the percentage of overweight or obese individuals, with 64% of the population affected. This figure is higher than the OECD average of 59%, which highlights the growing health issue facing the country.
